What are CEREC same-day crowns and how do they work?
CEREC same-day crowns are dental crowns designed, milled, and placed in a single appointment using CAD/CAM technology. Your dentist takes a digital scan of your tooth (no messy impressions), designs the crown on a computer, and mills it from a block of high-strength porcelain right in the office. The entire process takes about 90 minutes, and you leave with your permanent crown -- no temporary crown and no second visit needed.

What is dental Botox for TMJ and does it really work?
Dental Botox is a therapeutic treatment where small amounts of botulinum toxin are injected into the masseter (jaw) muscles to relieve TMJ pain, jaw tension, and teeth grinding (bruxism). It works by relaxing the overactive muscles that cause clenching and grinding. Most patients experience significant relief within 1-2 weeks, and results typically last 3-4 months. Our dentists at Family Orlando Dentistry are trained in therapeutic Botox administration.

What is the difference between CEREC crowns and traditional crowns?
CEREC crowns are completed in a single visit (about 90 minutes) using digital scanning and in-office milling, while traditional crowns require two visits and a 2-3 week wait while a lab fabricates the crown. With CEREC, there is no need for temporary crowns, messy impressions, or multiple injections. Both options use high-quality materials and are comparably priced at $800-$1,500. The main advantage of CEREC is convenience and precision.

Professional teeth whitening performed at Family Orlando Dentistry is safe and does not damage your tooth enamel when done correctly. You may experience temporary sensitivity, which typically resolves within 24-48 hours.
What should I do if I knock out a tooth?
If you knock out a permanent tooth, handle it by the crown (the white part), not the root. Rinse it gently without scrubbing. If possible, place it back in the socket and hold it in place. If you cannot reinsert it, place the tooth in a glass of milk or saliva to keep it moist. Call Family Orlando Dentistry immediately at (407) 877-9003. Time is critical -- the best outcomes occur when the tooth is reimplanted within 30 minutes.
Can Botox help with teeth grinding (bruxism)?
Yes, therapeutic Botox is an effective treatment for teeth grinding (bruxism). When injected into the masseter muscles, Botox reduces the force of involuntary jaw clenching and grinding. This alleviates jaw pain, headaches, and excessive tooth wear. Treatment takes about 15 minutes and results last 3-4 months. Family Orlando Dentistry offers dental Botox for bruxism and TMJ at our Ocoee FL office.
